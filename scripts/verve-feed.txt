				<content:encoded><![CDATA[<p>Please be sure to have read the <a title="General Submission Guidelines" href="http://ncpverve.wordpress.com/2013/01/08/general-submission-guidelines/">general submission guidelines</a>, as well!</p>
<p>All writing submissions should:</p>
<ul>
<li>Be typed in English.</li>
<li>Not include clichés or trite plots. Avoid writing about common subjects (e.g. relationships) unless you are bringing something new and interesting to the topic.</li>
<li>Connect to Verve’s theme, “Among other Things,” through content, theme, tone, or some other characteristic.</li>
<li>Be proofread and refined.</li>
</ul>
<p>Additional suggestions:</p>
<ul>
<li>Try to use an intriguing but accurate vocabulary; search for words that describe what you are trying to say effectively and specifically. At the same time, do not force yourself to incorporate grand words like “tergiversation” and “pulchritude” just for the sake of sounding intelligent; your personal voice matters more.</li>
<li>Feel free to submit works that are unusual or ridiculous, as they are often quite creative.</li>
</ul>
<p>Prose works should be no more than seven pages double spaced in Times New Roman, 12 point font. Any form of poetry is accepted, but poems should not exceed four pages in length. If you have a longer piece, you may still submit it, but it will likely have to be cut down in the editing process if chosen for publication.</p>
<p>The two categories for prose works are fiction and creative nonfiction. Fiction is entirely imagined, stories that have not actually happened, whereas creative nonfiction is based on true incidents, such as childhood memories, high school experiences, or current events. Despite this difference, the genres are quite similar; both include plot, character development, description, themes, and emotional weight. In addition, they can take many different forms: short story, letter, monologue, script, essay, etc. The only absolute requirement is a creative idea.</p>

To do so, send an email to <a href="mailto:ncp.verve@gmail.com">ncp.verve@gmail.com</a> with your work attached, including in the body of the email your name, your advisory, and your piece’s title and genre (fiction, creative nonfiction, poetry, visual arts, or photography). Also make the subject of your email the genre and title of your work (e.g. Poetry – The Road Not Taken). By submitting, you grant Verve permission to reproduce your work in print and/or online. You may submit as many works in as many genres as you wish. All submissions are due on Monday, March 18th. Pieces submitted after the submissions deadline will not be considered.</p>
<p>Once the submissions deadline passes, the Verve staff will use a blind procedure to evaluate all submissions anonymously. The Editors-in-Chief will make final decisions about which pieces to include in the print magazine and contact the artists who created them. The section editors will then communicate with the artists online or in person to refine the pieces as much as possible before publication. In this way, the Verve staff hopes to improve submissions with the help of their creators and without losing the original intent of the pieces. Submissions that for any reason are not chosen for the print magazine may be published online. Verve editors will try to meet with the artists of these pieces, as well, but if time does not allow, they may edit these works independently.</p>
<p>Please abide by the additional guidelines for <a title="Writing Submission Guidelines" href="http://ncpverve.wordpress.com/2013/01/08/writing-submission-guidelines/">writing</a> and <a title="Visual Arts/Photography Submission Guidelines" href="http://ncpverve.wordpress.com/2013/01/08/visual-submission-guidelines/">visual arts/photography</a> when creating and submitting your work. We look forward to receiving your submissions!</p>
